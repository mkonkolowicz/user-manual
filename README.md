# User Manual For Working with Maciek

Tips and tricks for working with me.

**Pronouns:** he/him/his

## My Values

- Honesty
- Tranparency
- Grit
- Care for Others

## What Motivates Me

- My People. I care a lot about building relationships within my teams and building strong trusting teams. I believe that my teams are or can be the best of the best. The team that everyone wants to join.
- Consistent improvement. I thrive when I see my people, and myself getting better at whatever we are doing.
- Being given a clear mission. A clear problem, and an understanding of what the pain point is. If one doesn't exist, or is ambiguous, I will first question why the mission exists.
- Working on problems which seem not important, but really are the grease on the wheels that keep the product moving forward. Maybe this is why I've been focused on non-functional requirements my entire career. 

## Me as a Manager

- "You'll see it when you believe it". This "ISM" is stolen from a past workplace of mine where I formed a lot of my leadership (Rocket Mortage). A lot of problems are hard , grueling and will cause pain. My best way to get through them, is to stay positive and see the best in the situation, without pretending it doesn't suck.
- "Push decisions out to the edge. I think the people who are closest to the work are the best equipped to make the right decisions, and my job is to give them as much context, information, and strategy insight as possible so they can make the right decision". I picked up this habit from one of my VPs. I am not the smartest person in the room, and while I can be a part of the decision, I think my job is to support the decision of the expert. 
- "Treat people like adults. Give them all the information, even if it's bad, and help them process it. If people will react immaturely to something, give them the information anyway, and coach them on their reaction". Another thing I picked up from one of my VPs (same one as above). She was really good at doing this, and I experienced how motivational this method of operation can be. 
- Don't act like an asshole. Belittling, hidden agendas, passive aggresive behaviour, or communication which can be interpreted as such is something I think is a cancer on a team, and I have a very short fuse for.  

## Triggers (How to make me annoyed or at least change facial expression to indicate disdain)
- Passive aggressive behaviour
- Arrogance
- Bureaucracy
- Judging people based on anything but their abilities
- Not doing what you say you are going to, without acknowledging it
- Ghosting during communication
- Throwing problems over the wall. We are the they.
  
If you notice me getting annoyed, it's best to call it out right away, to air out the friction.

## Tips and Tricks

- I want to hear painpoints, and what you think should be changed. I love collaborating with all different types of people in all types of different roles 
- I appreciate feedback. I also appreciate that feedback is hard to give, so don't wait to give feedback until a pre-planned session. It doesn't have to be perfect, and I like talking through it
- I like giving feedback as I see the opportunity to arise. If I'm giving you feedback, it's because I want you to improve.
- Once a person loses my trust, because I feel they are being repetetively neglectful, intentionally harmful, or negligent...I create less time for them in my life, and eventually push them out
- I highly value self-discipline, self awareness, and processes to enable the latter
- If you break my trust by accident, it is easy to repair it if you acknowledge it and let me know the reasoning 
- I love creating sneaky secondary solutions, if our primary problem solving plan is not going to work out
- I spent a lot of my formative years in a military like environment. I respect the chain of command, but also understand that it sometimes needs  to be operated around. I love when my teams make decisions without asking me for permissions, but instead including me in the discussion as an equal partner 
- I love outdoorsy stuff. I own two boats (sail and power), race one of them, fish, hunt and in general feel comfortable in the woods or lake
- I also love my family. I have three gorgeous children and a beautiful wife. Any time I spend on something other than them, is precious, needs to be accounted for and valuable.
